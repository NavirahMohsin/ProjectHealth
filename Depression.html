Depression (major depressive disorder) is a common and serious mental disorder that negatively affects how you feel, think, act, and perceive the world.
Depression

Causes and Symptoms:

  1. Causes:

Depression can happen to anyone. People who have lived through abuse, severe losses or other stressful events are more likely to develop depression. Women are more likely to have depression than men.A depressive episode can be categorized as mild, moderate, or severe depending on the number and severity of symptoms, as well as the impact on the individual’s functioning. Depression can, in turn, lead to more stress and dysfunction and worsen the affected person’s life situation and the depression itself.

  1. Symptoms:

The symptoms of depression can vary slightly depending on the type and can range from mild to severe. In general, symptoms include:Feeling very sad, hopeless or worried, Being easily irritated or frustrated, Eating too much or too little, which may result in weight gain or weight loss, Trouble sleeping (insomnia) or sleeping too much (hypersomnia), or Fatigue.
