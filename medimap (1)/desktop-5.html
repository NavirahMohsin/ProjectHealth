Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

Hypertension, commonly known as high blood pressure, is a chronic medical condition characterized by elevated blood pressure in the arteries. Blood pressure is the force exerted by circulating blood against the walls of the blood vessels.

  Causes and Symptoms

Causes: Primary hypertension has no identifiable cause and develops gradually over time. Factors that can contribute to its development include:

  1. Genetics: Family history of hypertension significantly increases the risk.
  2. Age: The risk of developing hypertension increases as you age.
  3. Race: Hypertension is particularly common and often more severe among people of African descent.
  4. Gender: Men are more likely to develop hypertension before age 64, while women are more likely to develop it after age 65.
  5. Dietary Factors: High sodium (salt) intake can lead to water retention, increasing blood pressure. Conversely, low potassium intake can increase blood pressure.

symptoms: Headaches: Often in the morning, located at the back of the head.

  1. Shortness of Breath: Difficulty breathing, especially during physical activity.
  2. Nosebleeds: Frequent nosebleeds without other apparent causes.
  3. Flushing: Reddening of the face.
  4. Dizziness: Feeling lightheaded or faint.
