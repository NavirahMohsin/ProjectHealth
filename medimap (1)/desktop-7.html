Cholera

Cholera is a severe bacterial infection caused by Vibrio cholerae, which primarily affects the small intestine. It's typically transmitted through contaminated water or food. The main symptom is profuse watery diarrhea, which can lead to dehydration and, if untreated, can be fatal within hours.

 

  Causes and Symptoms

Causes: Cholera is caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae, specifically strains belonging to serogroups O1 and O139. These bacteria typically thrive in water sources contaminated with feces from infected individuals. Transmission commonly occurs through the consumption of contaminated food or water.

symptoms: The primary symptom of cholera is profuse watery diarrhea, often described as "rice-water stool" due to its appearance. This diarrhea can lead to rapid dehydration, electrolyte imbalances, and shock if not promptly treated. Other symptoms may include vomiting, muscle cramps, and a rapid heart rate. In severe cases, individuals may develop sunken eyes, dry mucous membranes, cold and clammy skin, and a loss of consciousness.Cholera symptoms can appear rapidly, usually within hours to days after infection, depending on the ingested dose of bacteria. Some individuals may experience mild or asymptomatic infections, while others may develop severe symptoms requiring urgent medical attention.
