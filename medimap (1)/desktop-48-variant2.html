Japanese Encephalitis

Japanese encephalitis (JE) is a viral infection caused by the Japanese encephalitis virus (JEV), which belongs to the Flavivirus genus. It is primarily transmitted through the bite of infected mosquitoes, particularly species of the Culex genus, which breed in areas with stagnant water such as rice fields and swamps.

  Causes and Symptoms

Causes: Japanese encephalitis is endemic in many parts of Asia, particularly in rural and agricultural areas where there is close proximity between humans and animals, and where mosquito breeding habitats are abundant. The virus primarily affects the central nervous system, leading to inflammation of the brain (encephalitis).

symptoms: Symptoms of Japanese encephalitis can range from mild flu-like symptoms to severe neurological manifestations, including high fever, headache, neck stiffness, disorientation, seizures, paralysis, and coma. In severe cases, Japanese encephalitis can result in permanent brain damage or death.
