Treatment:

Treatment for iron deficiency anemia aims to replenish iron stores and restore normal hemoglobin levels. This often involves:

  • Iron supplementation: Oral iron supplements are commonly prescribed to increase iron levels. In severe cases or when oral supplementation is not effective, intravenous iron may be administered.
  • Dietary changes: Consuming iron-rich foods such as red meat, poultry, fish, beans, lentils, spinach, and fortified cereals can help improve iron levels.
  • Addressing underlying causes: Treatment may also involve addressing any underlying conditions contributing to iron deficiency, such as gastrointestinal bleeding or chronic inflammation.

Top Treatment Methods used by the world:

1. Iron supplements (ferrous sulfate, ferrous gluconate)

2. Vitamin C supplements (to enhance iron absorption)

3. Folate supplements (if deficient)

4. Vitamin B12 supplements (if deficient)

5. Blood transfusions (in severe cases)
