Treatment:

treatment for chronic bronchitis aims to relieve symptoms, prevent exacerbations, and slow the progression of the disease. This may involve:

  • Smoking cessation: Quitting smoking is the most important step in managing chronic bronchitis and slowing its progression. Smoking cessation programs and support groups can be helpful.
  • Medications: Bronchodilators, which help open the airways, and inhaled corticosteroids may be prescribed to relieve symptoms and reduce inflammation.
  • Pulmonary rehabilitation: This comprehensive program includes exercise training, education, and support to improve lung function and overall quality of life.
  • Oxygen therapy: Supplemental oxygen may be prescribed for individuals with severe chronic bronchitis and low blood oxygen levels.
  • Vaccinations: Annual flu vaccines and pneumococcal vaccines are recommended to reduce the risk of respiratory infections, which can exacerbate chronic bronchitis.

Top Treatment Methods used by the world:

1. Bronchodilators (e.g., salbutamol)

2. Inhaled corticosteroids

3. Oral corticosteroids

4. Antibiotics

5. Mucolytics
