Otitis externa (swimmer’s ear)

Causes and Symptoms:

  1. Causes:

causes:Swimmer’s ear (also called otitis externa) is an ear infection in your ear canal, the pathway between your outer ear and your middle ear. Swimmer’s ear may be a bacterial infection or fungal infection. It’s called swimmer’s ear because many avid swimmers develop the condition at some point in their lives.

  1. Symptoms:

symptoms: 1.ear pain: You may have an earache or notice ear pain if you pull or tug on your ear. Ear pain often gets worse if the underlying infection isn’t treated. Sometimes, the pain may spread from your ear to the side of your face. 2.itchiness inside of your ear. 3.Blocked ear. This feels like something is in your ear or a sense of fullness in your ear. 4.Redness and swelling in your outer ear.
