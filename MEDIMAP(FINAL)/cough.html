Tussis (cough)

Causes and Symptoms:

  1. Causes:

A cough, also known as tussis, is a voluntary or involuntary act that clears the throat and breathing passage of foreign particles, microbes, irritants, fluids, and mucus; it is a rapid expulsion of air from the lungs.Coughing can be done deliberately or as part of a reflex. Although coughing can be a sign of a serious illness, more often, it will clear up on its own without the need for medical attention.

  1. Symptoms:

Other reasons to seek medical advice include if the cough is getting worse, if there are swelling or lumps present in the neck region, if there is unexplained weight loss, if the coughing is severe, if there is difficulty swallowing or breathing, if there is chest pain, or if fevers are not getting better. These symptoms can indicate a more serious underlying condition and should be evaluated by a healthcare professional.
