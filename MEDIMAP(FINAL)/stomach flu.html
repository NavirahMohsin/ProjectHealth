Gastroenteritis (stomach flu)

Causes and Symptoms:

  1. Causes:

Viral gastroenteritis is an intestinal infection that includes signs and symptoms such as watery diarrhea, stomach cramps, nausea or vomiting, and sometimes fever.The most common way to develop viral gastroenteritis often called stomach flu is through contact with an infected person or by consuming contaminated food or water. If you're otherwise healthy, you'll likely recover without complications. But for infants, older adults and people with compromised immune systems, viral gastroenteritis can be deadly.

  1. Symptoms:

Although commonly referred to as stomach flu, gastroenteritis is not the same as influenza. While the flu (influenza) affects only the respiratory system—your nose, throat, and lungs—gastroenteritis targets your intestines, causing signs and symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, stomach cramps, pain, and occasional muscle aches or headaches. Understanding the difference between these two illnesses is important for proper diagnosis and treatment.
