Acne vulgaris is the formation of comedones, papules, pustules, nodules, and/or cysts as a result of obstruction and inflammation of pilosebaceous units (hair follicles and their accompanying sebaceous gland). Acne develops on the face and upper trunk. It most often affects adolescents.
Acne vulgaris

Causes and Symptoms:

Acne develops when hair follicles become clogged with oil (sebum) and dead skin cells. Several factors contribute to this process, including:
  • Excess sebum production: Hormonal changes during puberty, menstrual cycles, pregnancy, or certain medical conditions can increase sebum production.
  • Clogged pores: Dead skin cells and sebum can accumulate in hair follicles, leading to the formation of comedones (blackheads and whiteheads).
  • Bacterial overgrowth: Propionibacterium acnes, a type of bacteria normally present on the skin, can multiply within clogged pores and contribute to inflammation.
  • Inflammation: The body's immune response to bacterial overgrowth and trapped sebum can lead to redness, swelling, and the formation of papules, pustules, nodules, or cysts.
