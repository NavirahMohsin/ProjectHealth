Osteoarthritis (OA)

Osteoarthritis (OA) is the most common form of arthritis, primarily affecting the joints. It's a chronic condition characterized by the breakdown of cartilage, the tissue that covers the ends of bones in a joint.

Causes and Symptoms:

  1. Causes: The exact cause of OA isn't fully understood, but it's believed to involve a combination of genetic, mechanical, and environmental factors. Aging is a significant risk factor, as cartilage naturally wears down over time. Other risk factors include joint injury, obesity, repetitive stress on the joints, and certain metabolic disorders.
  2. Symptoms: Common symptoms of osteoarthritis include pain (especially after activity or at the end of the day), stiffness, swelling, and decreased range of motion in the affected joint. Symptoms typically worsen over time, but they can be managed with treatment.
