Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is a progressive lung disease that makes it difficult to breathe. It is characterized by long-term respiratory symptoms and airflow limitation due to airway and/or alveolar abnormalities, typically caused by significant exposure to noxious particles or gases.

  Causes and Symptoms

  • Primary Cause: Long-term exposure to harmful substances, most notably cigarette smoke. Other factors include air pollution, occupational dust and chemicals, and frequent lower respiratory infections during childhood.
  • symptoms:Persistent cough with mucus production (chronic bronchitis)
  • Shortness of breath, especially with physical exertion
  • Wheezing
  • Chest tightness
  • Frequent respiratory infections
  • Fatigue
  • Cyanosis (bluish tint to the lips or fingernail beds due to low oxygen levels)
