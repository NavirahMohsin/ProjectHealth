Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic medical condition that affects how your body converts food into energy. When you eat, your body breaks down carbohydrates into glucose, which is a type of sugar used by cells for energy. Insulin, a hormone produced by the pancreas, helps glucose enter your cells.

  Causes and Symptoms

  • In diabetes, the body either doesn't produce enough insulin (Type 1 diabetes), or it can't effectively use the insulin it produces (Type 2 diabetes). There are also other types of diabetes, such as gestational diabetes which occurs during pregnancy.
  • Type 1 diabetes is usually diagnosed in children and young adults and requires daily insulin injections. It occurs when the body's immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas.
  • Type 2 diabetes is more common and typically develops in adults, though it's becoming increasingly prevalent in children due to rising obesity rates. It's often linked to lifestyle factors like obesity, lack of physical activity, and poor diet. In Type 2 diabetes, the body becomes resistant to insulin, and the pancreas may not produce enough insulin to overcome this resistance.
  • Both types of diabetes can lead to high blood sugar levels, which, if left untreated, can cause serious complications such as heart disease, stroke, kidney disease, nerve damage, and vision problems.
