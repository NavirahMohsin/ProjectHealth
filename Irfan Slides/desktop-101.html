Treatment

Diagnosis of leptospirosis is based on clinical symptoms, as well as laboratory tests such as blood tests and polymerase chain reaction (PCR) to detect the presence of the bacteria. Treatment typically involves antibiotics, such as doxycycline or penicillin, which are most effective when started early in the course of the illness. Supportive care, including hydration, rest, and management of symptoms, may also be necessary, especially in severe cases.

Prevention of leptospirosis focuses on avoiding exposure to contaminated water or soil and minimizing contact with potentially infected animals. This includes wearing protective clothing, such as gloves and boots, when working or engaging in activities in high-risk environments, practicing good hygiene, and avoiding swimming or wading in potentially contaminated water.

Top Products used by the world:

1. Antibiotics (e.g., doxycycline)

2. Penicillin

3. Ceftriaxone

4. Intravenous fluids

5. Symptomatic treatment for fever and pain
