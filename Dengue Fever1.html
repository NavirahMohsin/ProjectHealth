Dengue (break-bone fever) is a viral infection that spreads from mosquitoes to people. It is more common in tropical and subtropical climates. Most people who get dengue will not have symptoms. But for those who do, the most common symptoms are high fever, headache, body aches, nausea, and rash.
Dengue Fever

Causes and Symptoms:

  1. Causes:

Dengue fever is transmitted to humans through the bite of infected female Aedes mosquitoes. These mosquitoes typically bite during the daytime, particularly in the early morning and late afternoon. Dengue is not directly spread from person to person, but rather through the bite of an infected mosquito.

  1. Symptoms:

Symptoms:-

  • High fever, often reaching 104°F (40°C)
  • Severe headache, typically located behind the eyes
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Muscle and joint pain, often described as severe
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Rash, which may appear 2 to 5 days after the onset of fever
  • Mild bleeding, such as nosebleeds or easy bruising
