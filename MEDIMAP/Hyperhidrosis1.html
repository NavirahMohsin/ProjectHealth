Hyperhidrosis (hi-pur-hi-DROE-sis) is excessive sweating that's not always related to heat or exercise. You may sweat so much that it soaks through your clothes or drips off your hands.
Hyperhidrosis (excessive sweating)

Causes and Symptoms:

  1. Causes:

  • Primary hyperhidrosis: This type occurs without an identifiable underlying cause and often begins during childhood or adolescence. It typically affects specific areas of the body, such as the palms, soles, or underarms.
  • Secondary hyperhidrosis: This type is caused by an underlying medical condition or medication side effect. It tends to cause more generalized sweating and may develop later in life.

  1. Symptoms:

  • Persistent cough that produces mucus (sputum) on most days for at least three months in two consecutive years
  • Excessive sweating that occurs regardless of temperature or physical activity
  • Sweat that drips off the hands or feet
  • Wet or clammy palms
  • Stained clothing or noticeable sweat marks
  • Skin maceration (softening and whitening of the skin) in areas prone to sweating
  • Social embarrassment or avoidance of certain activities due to sweating
