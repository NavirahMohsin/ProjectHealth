Treatment:

Treatment options for hyperhidrosis may include:

  • Topical antiperspirants: Over-the-counter or prescription-strength antiperspirants containing aluminum chloride can help reduce sweating when applied to affected areas.
  • Oral medications: Certain medications, such as anticholinergics or beta-blockers, may be prescribed to decrease sweating by blocking nerve signals to the sweat glands
  • Botulinum toxin injections: Injections of botulinum toxin (Botox) can temporarily block the release of acetylcholine, a neurotransmitter that stimulates sweat glands, effectively reducing sweating in targeted areas.
  • Iontophoresis: This treatment involves passing a mild electrical current through water-soaked skin to temporarily block sweat glands' activity, typically used for treating palm and foot hyperhidrosis.
  • Surgical options: In severe cases that do not respond to other treatments, surgical procedures such as sweat gland removal (sympathectomy) or sweat gland suction may be considered.

2. Botulinum toxin injections
