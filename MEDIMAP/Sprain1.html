A sprain is an injury to the ligaments around a joint. Ligaments are strong, flexible fibers that hold bones together. When a ligament is stretched too far or tears, the area around the joint will become painful and swell.
Sprain (ligament injury)

Causes and Symptoms:

  1. Causes:

Sprains typically occur when a joint is forced beyond its normal range of motion, often during activities such as sports, exercise, or accidental falls. Common causes include: Twisting or turning forcefully Landing awkwardly after a jump Direct impact to the joint Overstretching during physical activity

  1. Symptoms:

Symptoms of a sprain may vary depending on the severity but often include:

  • Pain, especially with movement or pressure on the affected joint
  • Swelling and tenderness around the injured area
  • Bruising or discoloration of the skin
  • Instability or feeling that the joint is "loose"
  • Limited range of motion
